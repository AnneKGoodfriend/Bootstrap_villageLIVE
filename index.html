About the Village:

Greenwich Village (also known as The Village) is thought of as the birthplace of the modern LGBTQI movement and continues to be an important cultural center for the LGBTQI community.

The Stonewall Riots took place on June 28th, 1969 at the Stonewall Inn, New York’s most popular gay bar at the time. On that fateful night, police raided the bar and targeted patrons based on their gender expression and sexuality. Members of the queer community had had enough, and they decided to fight back against the law enforcement’s homophobic and transphobic policies. The violence escalated and crowds gathered as the community asserted its collective right to exist.

The riots, which began as a spontaneous reaction to injustice, are widely considered one of the most important events leading to the gay liberation movement and the fight for equality for LGBTQI Americans. Today, the Stonewall Inn is a registered National Historic Landmark in recognition of its importance to the movement.

About Nelson:

Nelson Sullivan was a video artist who lived in Greenwich Village in the 1980s. He is widely recognized as the first vlogger, and his documentation of the city’s downtown scene is an unparalleled archive of queer life. His work features the likes of RuPaul, Keith Haring, Lady Bunny, Michael Alig, and Michael Musto. Thanks to the 5 Ninth Project, Nelson’s work has been digitized and organized on Youtube.
