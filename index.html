About VillageLIVE

This project aims to celebrate queer community in the ways that it shapes and is shaped by public space in New York City. Using Nelson Sullivan’s video archive, past narratives come back to life in the streets where prominent figures of the city’s queer history once flourished. The rainbow of experiences that make up queer life in New York is endlessly broad and beautiful, but here we begin with the story of one man.

About the Village

Greenwich Village (also known as The Village) is thought of as the birthplace of the modern LGBTQI movement and continues to be an important cultural center for the LGBTQI community. The Stonewall Riots took place on June 28th, 1969 at the Stonewall Inn, New York’s most popular gay bar at the time. The riots, which began as a spontaneous reaction to injustice, are widely considered one of the most important events leading to the gay liberation movement and the fight for equality for LGBTQI Americans.

About Nelson

Nelson Sullivan was a video artist who lived in Greenwich Village in the 1980s. He is widely recognized as the first vlogger, and his documentation of the city’s downtown scene is an unparalleled archive of queer life. His work features the likes of RuPaul, Keith Haring, Lady Bunny, Michael Alig, and Michael Musto. Thanks to the 5 Ninth Project, Nelson’s work has been digitized and organized on Youtube.
