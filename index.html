Meet Nelson

Nelson Sullivan was a video artist who lived in Greenwich Village in the 1980s. Nelson became fascinated with video, which was entering consumers’ hands with the advent of handheld camcorders. He is considered the first vlogger; he turned the camera on himself and assiduously documented his life in the queer downtown scene of New York, featuring a cast of queer characters including Rupaul, Keith Haring, Lady Bunny, and more. Through the thousands of hours of his footage, a picture is painted of a city in which queer life was supportive, vibrant, and proud to be in public.Thanks to the 5 Ninth Project, Nelson’s work has been digitized and organized on Youtube.

Why Augmented Reality?

Our hope is to create tools which enable people to reconnect with each other and with their past, to teach and learn from each other while essentially “walking through history.” We believe in the capacity of augmented reality and feel strongly about its ability to foster the creation and sharing of dynamic content. To us, this is what truly constitutes meaningful interaction and invites repeated, sustained engagement with users.

Why VillageLIVE?

This project aims to celebrate queer community in the ways that it shapes and is shaped by public space in New York City. Using Nelson Sullivan’s video archive, past narratives come back to life in the streets where prominent figures of the city’s queer history once flourished. The rainbow of experiences that make up queer life in New York is endlessly broad and beautiful, but here we begin with the story of one man.

Why the Village?

Greenwich Village (also known as The Village) is thought of as the birthplace of the modern LGBTQI movement and continues to be an important cultural center for the LGBTQI community. The Stonewall Riots took place on June 28th, 1969 at the Stonewall Inn, New York’s most popular gay bar at the time. The riots, which began as a spontaneous reaction to injustice, are widely considered one of the most important events leading to the gay liberation movement and the fight for equality for LGBTQI Americans.
